Buyer's guide

What to Look for in a Whey Protein

🏅

Third-Party Certification

Informed Choice, NSF Certified for Sport, or Informed Protein certification means an independent lab has verified the label claims and tested for banned substances. Non-negotiable for serious users.

🧬

Isolate vs Concentrate

Isolate is filtered further — 90%+ protein, lower lactose, fewer carbs and fat. Better for cutting and sensitive stomachs. Concentrate is cheaper and works well for bulking. Hydrolyzed isolate is the fastest absorbing of all.

📊

Protein per 100g

Marketing often leads with serving size, not concentration. Look for 70g+ protein per 100g of powder. This is the true measure of quality — not what the serving size is engineered to show.

🌿

Ingredient List

Shorter is better. Avoid protein powders with amino spiking (taurine, glycine listed in the protein blend) — this artificially inflates protein readings without providing muscle-building amino acids.
