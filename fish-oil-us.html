Frequently Asked Questions

This is the single most important technical distinction in fish oil. Triglyceride (TG) form is how omega-3 naturally exists in fish — your body absorbs it 70% more efficiently than ethyl ester (EE) form. Ethyl ester is cheaper to produce and used in most budget fish oils. Every product on this list uses triglyceride form. Never buy an ethyl ester fish oil unless price is the only consideration.
For general health maintenance: 500–1,000mg combined EPA+DHA daily. For cardiovascular health or inflammation management (as recommended by AHA): 1,000–2,000mg daily. Therapeutic dosing under medical supervision may reach 4,000mg. All products on this list support 1,000mg+ per serving, meeting cardiovascular maintenance dosing in 1–2 capsules daily.
Rancid fish oil smells strongly fishy or "off" even before opening — not just after. Oxidised omega-3 may actually be harmful rather than beneficial. This is why IFOS certification matters — it measures oxidation levels (TOTOX score), not just contamination. Products that score 5-stars on IFOS have passed strict oxidation tests. Always store fish oil in a cool, dark place and refrigerate after opening.
