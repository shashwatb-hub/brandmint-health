Know what you're buying

EPA vs DHA — What's the Difference?

Both omega-3s matter, but for different reasons. Choose based on your primary health goal.

EPA — Eicosapentaenoic Acid
Anti-inflammatory

EPA is the omega-3 primarily responsible for reducing inflammation. It competes with arachidonic acid to produce less inflammatory compounds throughout the body.

Reducing joint pain and stiffness
Lowering triglyceride levels

DHA — Docosahexaenoic Acid
Structural & cognitive

DHA is the primary structural omega-3 in brain tissue and the retina. It's critical for brain development and ongoing cognitive function throughout life.

Buyer's guide

What "High Purity" Actually Means

Four things to check before buying any fish oil in India.

Most Important
🧪

Third-Party Certification

IFOS (International Fish Oil Standards) is the global benchmark — every certified batch is tested by an independent lab for mercury, PCBs, dioxins, and freshness. Trustified and Clean Label are India-specific alternatives. Any brand that doesn't list a certification is unverified.

Affects Absorption
💧

Triglyceride vs Ethyl Ester

Triglyceride (TG) form is how omega-3 naturally exists in fish — it absorbs 50–70% better than the synthetically produced ethyl ester (EE) form used in cheaper products. Look for "triglyceride" or "TG form" on the label. TrueBasics and Carbamide Forte both use TG form.

Check the Label
📊

EPA+DHA vs Total Fish Oil

Many products advertise "1000mg fish oil" in big font — but the actual EPA+DHA content is far lower. A 1000mg capsule might only contain 300mg of active omega-3. Always check the EPA+DHA breakdown, not the total fish oil weight. Aim for 500mg+ combined EPA+DHA per capsule.

Freshness Indicator
⏱️

Oxidation (TOTOX Value)

Fish oil oxidizes over time, turning rancid. Rancid oil tastes and smells worse and may actually increase inflammation rather than reduce it. Fresh oil has a TOTOX value below 26. IFOS-certified products publish this. If a fish oil smells strongly fishy before you even open the capsule, it's likely oxidized.
